V and Inverted V curves of Synchronous Motor

1. A 3-phase synchronous generator is operating at a constant load while the excitation is adjusted to give unity pf current. If the excitation is now increased, the pf will
a) become leading
b) become lagging
c) remain at unity
d) become zero

2) A synchronous motor with negligible armature resistance runs at a load angle of 20⁰ at rated frequency. If supply frequency is increased by 10%, keeping other parameters constant, the new load angle will be
a) 16⁰
b) 18⁰
c) 20⁰
d) 22⁰

3) The constant speed of a synchronous motor can be changed to new fixed value by
a) changing the frequency of supply
b) changing the applied voltage
c) changing the load
d) interchanging any two phases

4) A synchronous motor working on leading power factor and not driving any mechanical, is known
a) synchronous induction motor
b) spinning motor
c) synchronous condenser
d) none of the above

5) In a synchronous motor if the back emf generated in the armature at no load is approximately equal to the applied voltage, then
a) the excitation is said to be zero percent
b) the motor is said to be fully loaded
c) the excitation is said to be 100%
d) the torque generated is maximum
